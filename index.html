Stories about the Children

The daily lives of Sri Lanka's citizens and children have improved significantly since the country's independence in 1948. Sri Lanka has made good progress in improving the lot of children all around the country, even in the face of the traumas caused by a civil war, a string of natural catastrophes, and the legacy of institutional, legal, and political change.

Children require a nutrient-rich diet and quality education to reach their full potential.

For children all around the nation to reach their full potential and completely fulfill their rights, assistance is still required. Extreme poverty is still present in some places, especially in the North, East, hill country, and several cities. Undernourishment, infant and under-five mortality, and unequal access to preventative and curative health services continue to be problems for vulnerable communities.
